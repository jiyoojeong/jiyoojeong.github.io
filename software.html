Special Relativity Research - International Baccalaureate

Abstract: Time is but a construct that has evolved with civilization: an indefinite and infinite material that man has decided to subdivide into hours, minutes, and seconds. For the longest time, we believed that time was inflexible. Jumping back and forth in time could only be done through the efforts of fictional narrators and sentimental mementos -- or can time be warped even in real life? Einstein in 1905 postulated relativistic time dilation: a phenomenon where time runs slowly when considering special relativity and general relativity based on Einstein’s two theories on relativity. This paper will further explore the validity of Einstein’s time dilation with the question “To what extent is Einstein’s time dilation postulate valid?”

2017

Orbital Optimization of Near-Earth Asteroids - Yale University Astronomy

Abstract: Near-Earth asteroids are one of the most-immediate threats to the Earth and humanity. Asteroids like the Chelyabinsk meteor that exploded over Russia in 2013 have caused significant damages in the area, while others like the Chicxulub asteroid that impacted the Earth 66 million years ago have altered the course of natural history. Asteroids such as 1998 YP11 have elliptical orbits that bring them close to the orbit of the Earth, making them potential impactors. We have taken a month of images of 1998 YP11 and modelled the asteroid's long term orbit using the Method of Gauss, a Runge-Kutta-4 integrator, a genetic algorithm optimizer, and a long-term integrator. We have calculated the celestial orbital elements eccentricity vector of a = 2.5275 au, e = 0.56530, i = 14.861°, Omega = 55.858°, and omega = 170.71°. Albedo and absolute magnitude were also measured in order to estimate the asteroid's size. Within the next 160,000 years, 1998 YP11's distance of closest approach is 0.0077430 AU, which will be in 48499 years. Though dangerously close, it will be at a distance four times the distance of the Moon to the Earth.
